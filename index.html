Where do they work?

The interactive chart below shows the range of sectors and subsectors of the economy, which employ our alumni. The industries are based on the North American Industry Classification System (NAICS), a standardized industry classification system used by federal statistical agencies. NAICS offers different levels of detail. NAICS 2 digit level provide a higher level of aggregation whereas NAICS 3 level offers narrower i.e. more detailed description. Numbers on the graph refer to the percentage of graduates employed in a particular sector or industry.

Data for this study were obtained from the Ohio Longitudinal Data Archive (OLDA). The OLDA is a data repository that is comprised of public administrative records on education, work, and training experiences of Ohio residents over time. The OLDA is a project of the Ohio Education Research Center .

Due to confidentiality restrictions, not all information can be displayed and therefore it is suppressed. It is important to remember this is a snapshot of our alumni. It is based on the number of students who graduated from Patton College between 2013 and 2016 and remained in Ohio. Graduates who left the state are therefore not included. Similarly, self-employed and those employed by the federal government are excluded.

Important Caveats

The chart on the left shows the percentage of graduates accepting employment within six months and twelve months. This information can be broken down by year and academic level. As before, the following restrictions apply:

  • the data include students who graduated between 2013 and 2016
  • graduates who left Ohio are not captured in this analysis; differences in employment rates between graduate and undegraduate students is due to the significant percentage of nonresident students at the graduate level (37% vs 8%), many of whom return to their home state or country after graduation.
  • the data include both full-time and part-time employment
  • self-employed and those employed by the federal government are excluded
